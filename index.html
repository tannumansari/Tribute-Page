Tribute to Savitribai Phule

Early Life

Savitribai Phule was born on January 3, 1831, in Naigaon, Satara district, Maharashtra, India. She was married to Jyotirao Phule at the age of nine, and together they worked tirelessly to promote education for women and lower castes.

Achievements

In 1848, Savitribai and Jyotirao Phule started the first indigenously-run school for girls in Pune. Despite facing tremendous opposition, she continued to work for the upliftment of women and the oppressed, establishing several schools, women's shelters, and a care center for pregnant rape victims.

Legacy

Savitribai Phule is remembered as India's first female teacher and a pioneer of women's education. Her efforts laid the foundation for the education of girls in India, and her legacy continues to inspire countless women and social reformers.

Timeline of Highlights

  • 1831: Born in Naigaon, Maharashtra.
  • 1848: Opened the first school for girls in Pune.
  • 1852: Started Mahila Seva Mandal to raise awareness about women's rights.
  • 1890: Established a care center for pregnant rape victims.
  • 1897: Passed away while serving people during a plague outbreak.
